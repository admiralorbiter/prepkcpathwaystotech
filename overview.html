  2. Client Connected Projects

In an effort to increase market value asset attainment in students before graduation, client connected projects are a scalable way to engage with students with authentic projects. These projects arm our students with essential skills needed to enter the workforce and above all create pockets of authentic learning that connect back to education standards.


  • Client
  • Students
  • Educator

Benefits

  • Access to underserved talent
  • Quality Work on Backburner Projects
  • Build relationships with students and community
  • Mentorship and Teaching has shown to improve worker success

Role of Client

The role of the clients can as hands on as they are able to manage. For some client projects, the client setups the project prompt and then has a few check ins before the final deliverable. For others, the client works with the students throughout the life of the project. There is no one size fits all approach to client connected projects

Benefits

  • Connect school standards to authentic real world projects
  • Meet professionals and explore different career types
  • Work on transferable skills that can be applied to all classes

Role of Student

The students job is to take the project tasks and work to complete it. Often times, students will be in charge of the whole process, from collecting requirements from the client to setitng the timeline and finally delvering the final artifact.

Benefits

  • Authentic engaging projects
  • Connect classroom concepts to skills used in the workforce

Role of Educator

The educator needs to step into the role of a facilitator. Especially concerning client projects, the educator may not be the main source of knowledge. Instead, the educator works as a project manager, making sure students are on task and connecting them with resources or professionals that will be able ot help students progress.
